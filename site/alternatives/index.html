Standalone vs bridges

Not a bridge to another app. The app itself.

Most personal finance MCP servers on GitHub are bridges: thin wrappers that connect your AI to an account you already pay for in YNAB, Monarch Money, Actual Budget or Firefly III. A bridge inherits the app behind it - the subscription, the onboarding, the hosting - and adds an API token to manage. MyFinance MCP is standalone: the server is the finance app.

MCP bridge to a finance app

  • Needs the underlying app: a paid subscription (YNAB, Monarch, Lunch Money) or a server you self-host (Actual, Firefly III)
  • Usually runs locally with your API token; you install, update and secure it yourself
  • Tracking still happens in the app; the AI is a remote control
  • Two accounts, two logins, two places where your data lives

MyFinance MCP, standalone

  • No other app required: expenses, budgets, trends and net worth live here
  • Hosted remote server with OAuth 2.1: paste the URL, sign in, done
  • The chat is the interface; dashboards render right in the conversation
  • One account, exportable to CSV any time, deletable with one tool call

Already happy with your finance app? Keep it - a bridge is the right tool for that. MyFinance MCP is for people who want the AI chat to be the whole finance stack.
